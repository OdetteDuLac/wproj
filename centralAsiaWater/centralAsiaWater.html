Waterways of Central Asia

In this project I experimented with mapping political boundaries with water rather than land in Central Asia. I included lakes, whose depletion is a major concern in the region as far as access to freshwater, agricultural irrigation and the continuation of fishing operations, which is historically important to local economies. Furthermore, I included dams, which play a significant role in the water geopolitics of the region, as countries compete for access to freshwater from rivers, particularly the Amu and Syr Daryas, which provide a large portion of water for agricultural irrigation. The map was done in a local projection.
